§ 01

About.

SZ

Deep learning models are confident, persuasive, and frequently wrong. My research builds the scaffolding that surrounds them — efficient algorithms with provable, distribution-free guarantees on uncertainty, coverage, and robustness.

I am a PhD researcher in Trustworthy AI, advised by Prof. Dr. Aleksandar Bojchevski at CISPA Helmholtz Center for Information Security and the University of Cologne. My work centers on conformal prediction, uncertainty quantification, and adversarial robustness — with a current obsession for graph-structured data and large language models.

Before academia, I shipped production ML systems: backend services for quantitative trading, electricity-market forecasting at national scale, and most recently — finetuning LLMs for speech translation at Apple. I am headed to Microsoft as an Applied Scientist intern in April 2026.

§ 02

Research.

§ 02.1Coverage

Conformal Prediction.

Building prediction sets with finite-sample, distribution-free coverage — for classification, regression, and graphs. The goal isn't accuracy. It's honesty under uncertainty.

§ 02.2Adversarial

Robustness, certified.

Single-certificate and single-sample techniques that provably bound model behavior under adversarial perturbations. No bluff. No empirical-only defenses. A receipt the math will sign.

§ 02.3Calibration

Uncertainty, quantified.

Calibrating epistemic and aleatoric uncertainty in deep models — with care taken for graph neural networks and LLMs. What does the model not know, and can we measure it?
