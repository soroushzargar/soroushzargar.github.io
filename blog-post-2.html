Reflections on NeurIPS 2025: One Sample is Enough

NeurIPS 2025 Conformal Prediction Robustness

NeurIPS 2025 in Vancouver was, in many ways, the most memorable conference of my PhD so far. This year we presented Efficient Adversarially Robust Conformal Prediction via One-Shot Resampling, a paper that started as a deceptively simple observation and grew into something I am genuinely proud of. Here are some reflections on the work and the experience.

The Core Idea

The standard approach to making conformal prediction robust against adversarial perturbations involves searching over worst-case inputs — an expensive procedure that scales poorly with perturbation budget and model complexity. Our key insight was that you do not need to explore the full adversarial neighborhood. A single, carefully chosen adversarial example per calibration point is sufficient to obtain tight coverage guarantees under attack.

The result is striking in its simplicity: by augmenting each calibration example with exactly one adversarial perturbation and adjusting the quantile accordingly, we match the coverage guarantees of far more expensive methods while reducing runtime by an order of magnitude. The technique generalizes across threat models — ℓ∞, ℓ2, and beyond — without any change to the core algorithm.

Why This Matters for Deployment

Previous work on robust conformal prediction tended to be theoretically elegant but practically slow. Running a full adversarial search at calibration time for tens of thousands of examples is feasible in a research setting, but it is a real barrier to adoption in systems that need to recalibrate frequently or operate under tight compute budgets.

Our one-shot approach makes robust calibration roughly as cheap as standard conformal prediction. This brings adversarially robust uncertainty quantification within reach for deployment scenarios where it matters most: autonomous systems, medical decision support, and real-time classification pipelines where both reliability and efficiency are non-negotiable.

The Conference Experience

Beyond the paper itself, NeurIPS 2025 was a great opportunity to connect with the rapidly growing conformal prediction community. The field has matured considerably — there were dedicated workshops, invited talks from pioneers of the theory, and a noticeable shift toward practical applications rather than purely theoretical contributions.

Several conversations at the poster session pushed my thinking in new directions. A recurring theme was the question of conditional coverage: the marginal guarantee that conformal prediction provides is powerful but sometimes insufficient. Guaranteeing coverage not just on average but conditionally — for every subgroup, every input region — remains an open and genuinely hard problem that I expect will occupy the community for years.

What Comes Next

One thread I am actively pursuing is the connection between robust conformal prediction and test-time adaptation. Most robustness work assumes a fixed, pre-trained model. But in many real deployments the model is updated or fine-tuned as new data arrives. How do coverage guarantees interact with a model that is itself changing? This is the question at the heart of my current work, and I am optimistic that the one-shot resampling idea will extend naturally to this more dynamic setting.
