Interspeech 2019 Speech Processing Alzheimer Detection

A Multi-Modal Feature Embedding Approach to Diagnose Alzheimer Disease from Spoken Language

Soroush H. Zargarbashi, Bagher BabaAli

Interspeech 2019

PDF

Abstract

Early detection of Alzheimer's disease from spontaneous speech analysis offers a non-invasive and cost-effective diagnostic pathway that could complement or reduce the need for expensive clinical assessments. We present a multi-modal feature embedding approach that jointly learns from acoustic, lexical, and semantic features extracted from speech recordings to distinguish Alzheimer's patients from healthy controls. By fusing heterogeneous feature types through a shared embedding space, our model captures complementary aspects of the cognitive decline that manifest in speech — including prosodic changes, word-finding difficulties, reduced vocabulary diversity, and semantic coherence degradation. Evaluated on the DementiaBank Pitt Corpus, our multi-modal approach achieves competitive classification performance while the learned attention weights provide interpretable insights into which linguistic and acoustic markers are most predictive of disease status.
