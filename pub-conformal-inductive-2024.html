ICLR 2024 Graph Neural Networks Conformal Prediction

Conformal Inductive Graph Neural Networks

Soroush H. Zargarbashi, Aleksandar Bojchevski

International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2024)

PDF arXiv Code

Abstract

Applying conformal prediction to graph neural networks is complicated by the fact that node predictions are not exchangeable — each node's representation depends on its neighborhood, creating complex statistical dependencies that invalidate the coverage guarantees of standard conformal methods. We develop conformal inductive graph neural networks that resolve this dependency problem through a careful separation of training, calibration, and inference phases. Our construction allows inductive GNNs to be used within the conformal prediction framework with a valid marginal coverage guarantee, by identifying and exploiting an approximate exchangeability structure that arises from the inductive setting. We also provide tools for understanding how the graph topology and message-passing depth affect coverage empirically, and validate our approach across multiple node classification benchmarks with various GNN architectures.
