Quantifying Epistemic Predictive Uncertainty in Conformal Prediction

Siu Lun Chau, Soroush H. Zargarbashi, Yusuf Sale, Michele Caprio

Under Review — International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2026)

PDF arXiv

Abstract

Conformal prediction provides marginal coverage guarantees but does not distinguish between uncertainty arising from model limitations (epistemic) and inherent data randomness (aleatoric). This distinction matters in practice: epistemic uncertainty signals inputs for which collecting more data or improving the model would reduce prediction uncertainty, while aleatoric uncertainty reflects irreducible noise. We develop a framework for quantifying epistemic predictive uncertainty within conformal prediction, enabling practitioners to identify predictions where model improvement would be most impactful. Our approach connects conformal theory with imprecise probability, providing theoretical guarantees on epistemic uncertainty quantification while maintaining the distribution-free coverage properties of classical conformal methods. Experiments on standard benchmarks demonstrate that our epistemic decomposition reveals meaningful structure in prediction sets beyond what marginal coverage alone provides.
